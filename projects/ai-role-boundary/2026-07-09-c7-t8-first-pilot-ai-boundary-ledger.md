## Boundary Under Review

The C7/T8 scaffolds already name allowed support, conditional support,
disallowed authority, and adversarial AI patterns. The newer first-pilot bundle
and capture-escalation ledger add a practical sequencing problem:

```text
Which AI concern can be repaired by making the review trace clearer,
which one means the first-pilot packet is still source-trace incomplete,
which one should be read as capture-review pressure,
and which one requires Joe or governed review before any response?
```

The safe current move is to preserve that classification. It is not to decide
AI-use policy, require disclosure, select tools, appoint reviewers, or run a
live AI-assisted review.

## Safe State Labels

A later review-prep pass can use these labels without adopting policy:

| label | meaning | safe consequence |
|---|---|---|
| `local_ai_trace_repair` | The issue can be clarified by adding AI task, source-check, decision-owner, non-authority, or boundary language to a draft artifact. | Edit draft review-prep artifacts only. |
| `source_trace_missing` | The issue cannot be assessed because target, source, contribution-unit, mechanism, limitation, or human-check fields are absent. | Keep the packet in revision-prep state. |
| `ai_capture_review_needed` | The AI issue may create volume, synthetic-consensus, prompt-shaping, citation-laundering, or reviewer-abdication pressure. | Preserve the concern as capture-review evidence without response policy. |
| `governed_ai_review_required` | The next response would change AI-use policy, reviewer authority, disclosure, sanctions, eligibility, rights, reward, governance, transfer, public posture, or live records. | Stop for Joe or governed review. |
| `do_not_use_live` | The packet would create institutional meaning before AI authority, source trace, and contestability are visible enough for review. | Do not launch, invite, review, log, score, sanction, or publish as process. |

These labels are review-prep vocabulary only. They are not contribution review
states, AI-use policy states, sanctions, rights markers, reward states,
governance states, transfer states, claim verdicts, or test results.

## Review Questions

1. What did AI do, if anything, and who checked the source material directly?
2. Is the final rationale attributable to a human or governed reviewer rather
   than model output?
3. Would a contributor understand and contest the decision without hidden
   prompts or private model traces?
4. Is any AI-generated source lead still clearly unverified?
5. Does AI output create synthetic consensus, volume pressure, citation
   laundering, prompt opacity, or reviewer abdication pressure?
6. Would a response require AI-use policy, disclosure, sanction, eligibility,
   reviewer authority, rights, reward, governance, transfer, public posture, or
   live-record movement?
7. Is the safest current state `source_trace_missing`,
   `ai_capture_review_needed`, or `do_not_use_live` rather than review-ready?
