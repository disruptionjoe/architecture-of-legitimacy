## Safe Rights Labels

These labels may be used in later review-prep handoffs without adopting policy:

| label | meaning | safe consequence |
|---|---|---|
| `local_rights_trace_repair` | The issue can be clarified by adding boundary, rationale, disputed-point, no-rights, or no-reward language to a draft artifact. | Edit draft review-prep artifacts only. |
| `identity_or_exit_policy_missing` | The issue cannot be assessed because identity, pseudonym, exit, withdrawal, correction, deletion, or retention policy is absent. | Keep the packet synthetic, provisional, or evidence-blocked. |
| `retained_record_boundary_missing` | The issue needs a stable record, review state, rationale, links, loss notes, or future-use boundary before it can be reviewed. | Preserve missing fields; do not create a live record. |
| `contestability_governed_stop` | The issue would become appeal, dispute, response-time, reviewer, reversal, or governance authority if answered. | Preserve the contested point and stop before live use. |
| `rights_reward_governed_stop` | The issue intersects participant rights, retained value, reward, score conversion, ownership, or governance standing. | Preserve no-current-rights/no-current-reward boundaries and route to Joe/governed review. |
| `public_or_live_record_blocked` | The next action would create public proof, pilot, invitation, contribution-log, contributor-record, or external-consequence meaning. | Stop; keep the artifact as review prep. |
| `do_not_use_live` | The packet would create rights, reward, identity, appeal, retention, governance, or public meaning before the boundaries are visible enough. | Do not launch, invite, review, log, score, retain, sanction, reward, transfer, or publish as process. |

These are review-prep labels only. They are not participant-rights states,
appeal states, contribution review states, reward states, identity states,
governance states, sanctions, claim verdicts, or test results.

## What This Adds To The RQ7 Rights Boundary

The earlier RQ7 first-pilot rights boundary established the fields a later
first-pilot packet must keep visible. This ledger adds a narrower operating
layer:

1. It classifies rights prompts as stop states rather than treating them as a
   generic second-ring warning.
2. It separates local wording repair from rights, identity, retention, appeal,
   reward, and governance movement.
3. It gives later review a compact state vocabulary that can block live use
   without changing policy.

It does not strengthen C5, mark T11 passed, adopt participant rights, make
Phase 1 closer, or show that the workflow is legitimate under real participant
pressure. It only makes the remaining rights stop conditions easier to inspect.
