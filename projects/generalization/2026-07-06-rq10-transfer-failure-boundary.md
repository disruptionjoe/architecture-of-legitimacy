# RQ10 Transfer Failure Boundary Map

Status: non-adopted research scaffold.

This map supplements the RQ10 transfer-conditions scaffold by naming when the
pattern should not transfer, should pause, or should stay horizon-only. It does
not claim that the repo has generalized, does not choose a pilot target, does
not adopt transfer policy, does not change C8 status, and does not create
reward, rights, sanction, disclosure, governance, portability, or public-posture
commitments.

## Boundary Question

RQ10 asks what must be true before this pattern can apply beyond one repo.

The first transfer question is not "where should this go next?" It is:

```text
What evidence would force us to say:
do not transfer this pattern yet,
do not transfer it to this context,
or do not describe this as generalization?
```

That question protects C8 from becoming an aspiration disguised as a result.

## Transfer Object Reminder

The transferable object is not:

- a complete institution;
- a governance package;
- a brand or public posture;
- a reward mechanism;
- a participant-rights regime;
- a sanctions system;
- or a claim that one repo has already solved legitimacy.

At most, the candidate transfer object is a coupled review pattern:

```text
inspectable contribution evidence
  -> contestable validation
  -> durable record
  -> explicit loss or residue
  -> deferred reward, rights, and governance gates
  -> capture monitoring as value accumulates
```

If that object cannot be stated without importing private founder judgment or
unreviewed governance machinery, RQ10 should remain a horizon claim.

## Failure Boundaries

| boundary | failure signal | immediate disposition | evidence needed before reopening |
|---|---|---|---|
| Source trace missing | The source repo has no outsider-readable accepted, revised, rejected, or contested contribution traces. | Pause transfer. | Public traces with rationale, loss notes, contestability marker, and log eligibility. |
| Private-context dependence | A newcomer cannot reconstruct why a decision was legitimate without founder explanation. | Treat the pattern as local-only. | Rationale checks showing the decision path is understandable from public records. |
| Target evidence mismatch | The target context cannot make contribution evidence inspectable without exposing private, regulated, or sensitive material. | Exclude that context for now. | A target-specific evidence substitute that preserves auditability without unsafe disclosure. |
| Reward-first pressure | The target needs payout, token, grant, ownership, or governance-weight promises before validation works. | Reject transfer as premature. | RQ9 reward readiness plus RQ8 capture review for the target pressure profile. |
| Rights/governance dependency | The target cannot function without participant rights, appeal rules, sanctions, or transferred powers that this repo has not justified. | Keep transfer blocked. | RQ6 and RQ7 review packets mature enough to separate rights, powers, and dispute paths. |
| Capture-positive scaling | Added value would make gaming or cartel behavior attractive faster than detection, mitigation, and review can mature. | Pause and route to attack modeling. | Target-specific attack-profitability notes and mitigation burden before pilot discussion. |
| Portability confusion | A reusable governance package is treated as if it were already proven portable. | Stop and preserve the distinction. | Separate portability evidence, origin inventory, adaptation review, and kill criteria. |
| Public-language inflation | The move would require saying the system "works," "generalizes," or is "ready" beyond the evidence. | Do not make the public claim. | Narrower wording that keeps C8 horizon-only and names unresolved burdens. |
| Process-drag failure | The target gains ceremony but not clearer legitimacy, contribution quality, or contestability. | Do not transfer the workflow. | Cost notes showing the process reduces ambiguity more than it increases overhead. |
| External-consequence boundary | Transfer would create legal, financial, reputational, client, civic, or external publishing consequences. | Joe/governance review required before action. | Explicit authorization and a target-specific risk review. |

## Excluded-Until-Review Contexts

These are not permanently invalid. They are excluded until the named review
burden is met.

| context | excluded until | reason |
|---|---|---|
| Funding allocation or grantmaking | RQ9 reward readiness and RQ8 capture modeling are target-specific. | Money converts weak review into high-value attack surface. |
| Token, equity, ownership, or governance-weight systems | Rights, reward, and transfer-power gates are explicit. | A contribution record can become an entitlement or control claim too quickly. |
| Regulated, confidential, legal, medical, or client-sensitive settings | Evidence handling can be inspected without unsafe disclosure. | Legitimacy needs visible reasons, but the target may not safely expose evidence. |
| Public civic decision processes | Authority, appeal, representation, and external-consequence review are explicit. | Civic use can turn research scaffolding into public power. |
| Cross-repo governance-package reuse | Portability evidence is separate from RQ10 generalization. | Process inventory is not proof that a governance package should travel. |
| High-volume open-source maintenance | Contribution volume, reviewer load, and non-code work are modeled. | Volume can make visible rationale too expensive or purely performative. |

## Minimum Review Packet Before Any Pilot Discussion

A later RQ10 review packet should include:

1. Source traces showing accepted, revised, rejected, and contested contribution
   paths, or a clear explanation of which path is still missing.
2. A target-context evidence map naming what can be public, what must stay
   private, and what substitute evidence would be acceptable.
3. A local-only component inventory separating portable protocol shape from
   source-repo taste, domain knowledge, and founder judgment.
4. A capture-pressure note that names how value, rewards, rights, or governance
   power would change attack incentives.
5. A reward and rights deferral statement for the target context.
6. A process-drag check showing why the workflow improves legitimacy rather
   than adding ceremony.
7. A public-language boundary keeping C8 horizon-only unless comparative
   evidence actually supports stronger wording.
8. Stop criteria for ending the pilot or rejecting the target before launch.

This packet would prepare review. It would not itself approve transfer.

## Relationship To Other RQ Gates

RQ10 should consume earlier gates without weakening them.

- RQ5/T3 tells whether the source workflow is usable enough to inspect.
- RQ6/T7 names governance powers and rollback pressures.
- RQ7 names participant voice, exit, retained-record, and dispute pressures.
- RQ8/T6 names capture and attack-profitability pressure.
- RQ9 names reward-readiness and no-reward baseline conditions.

If any of these gates are unresolved for the target context, RQ10 should record
that fact rather than route around it.

## Review Questions

Before any transfer discussion moves past research prep, a reviewer should be
able to answer:

1. What would make this target a bad fit even if the source repo improves?
2. Which parts of the source workflow are genuinely portable, and which are
   founder or domain judgment?
3. What would become less legitimate after transfer?
4. What new attack becomes profitable because the target accumulates more value?
5. Which public words are still forbidden because C8 remains horizon-only?
6. What concrete result would make the project stop the transfer attempt?

## Stop Conditions

Stop and require Joe/governance review before:

- naming or committing to a pilot target;
- claiming the pattern has generalized;
- changing C8 status or public posture;
- adopting transfer criteria as policy;
- transferring governance-package machinery to another repo;
- making reward, rights, sanction, disclosure, governance, appeal, or
  participant-entitlement commitments;
- taking legal, financial, civic, client, publishing, deployment, or other
  non-GitHub external action.
