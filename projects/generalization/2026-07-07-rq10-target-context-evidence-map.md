# RQ10 Target-Context Evidence Map

Status: non-adopted research scaffold.

This map turns the RQ10 transfer-conditions scaffold and transfer-failure
boundary into context-specific evidence needs for later review. It does not
claim that this repo has generalized, does not choose a pilot target, does not
adopt transfer policy, does not export governance-package machinery, does not
change C8 status, and does not create reward, rights, sanction, disclosure,
governance, portability, public-posture, or external-consequence commitments.

## Review Question

RQ10 should not ask "where should the pattern go next?" until the source repo
has visible contribution traces and the target context can preserve evidence
without creating unsafe commitments.

The narrower question for this map is:

```text
For each possible target context, what evidence would need to be public,
inspectable, and bounded before transfer could even be discussed?
```

The expected answer can be "not enough evidence yet." That is a valid research
result because C8 is a horizon claim, not an operational promise.

newcomer can inspect from public records alone.

That means every target context remains blocked before pilot selection. This
map can still improve RQ10 by naming what would be required later and what
should stay out of scope now.

## Target-Context Evidence Matrix

| target context type | public evidence needed before discussion | evidence hazard | required boundary before reopening |
|---|---|---|---|
| Adjacent research repo | A clear contribution unit, public rationale needs, candidate record surface, and evidence that local claims can be reviewed without founder context. | Local taste can masquerade as portable method. | Label portable, adaptable, and local-only pieces before any target is named. |
| Open-source maintainer project | Examples of code and non-code contribution records, maintainer rationale, issue or PR state transitions, and reviewer-load constraints. | Volume pressure can make visible rationale ceremonial or impossible. | Start with a bounded trace comparison, not a live maintainer workflow. |
| Public-goods funding process | Public proposal evidence, allocation criteria, reviewer rationale, dispute path, and no-reward or reward-deferred baseline. | Money makes weak review a high-value attack surface. | RQ9 reward readiness and RQ8 target-specific capture review must mature first. |
| Local civic or cooperative project | Inspectable contribution evidence, authority map, affected-party voice, and privacy-safe rationale surface. | Civic or cooperative use can create real public power or reputational consequence. | Joe/governance review plus target-specific external-consequence review before action. |
| Governance-package portability case | Origin inventory, adaptation boundaries, kill criteria, and proof that reuse improves legitimacy rather than ceremony. | Portability inventory can be mistaken for proof that governance machinery should travel. | Keep governance-package portability separate from RQ10 generalization unless reviewed. |
| Regulated or confidential setting | A safe evidence substitute that preserves auditability without exposing protected material. | Legitimacy needs visible reasons, but the target may not be able to expose evidence. | Exclude until evidence handling is designed and approved for the specific setting. |
| High-volume contribution environment | Sampling rules, reviewer-load budget, rationale minimums, escalation triggers, and failure-rate monitoring. | Process drag or automation can replace judgment with formal compliance. | Prove the process reduces ambiguity more than overhead before pilot discussion. |

## Portable Versus Local-Only Screen

A later RQ10 review packet should not treat the whole source repo as portable.
It should classify each component before target selection.

| component | likely portable shape | local-only or adaptable risk | evidence needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contribution evidence | Require public source, relevance, limitation, and source/inference separation. | Evidence norms vary by domain and sensitivity. | Target-context evidence map with privacy and auditability constraints. |
| Review rationale | Preserve decisive reason, missing evidence, and protected non-conclusions. | Rationale minimums can become too costly or too vague in high-volume contexts. | Source traces showing rationale can be written without private context. |
| Visible record | Keep durable state, attribution, validation, and loss notes inspectable. | Record surfaces vary by repo, platform, institution, and privacy need. | Record-surface comparison for the target context. |
| Contestability marker | Name what can be challenged and what was not decided. | Contestability can imply appeal rights or response-time promises. | RQ7 rights boundary and RQ4 cadence boundary before live use. |
| No-reward baseline | Separate record, recognition, useful residue, and future review from payout. | Target contexts may expect funding, tokens, ownership, or governance weight. | RQ9 no-reward baseline plus attack-delta review. |
| Capture note | Identify pressure variables and local repair versus escalation boundary. | Capture response can become sanction, disclosure, or eligibility policy. | RQ8 target-specific attack-profitability note before adoption. |
| AI assistance boundary | Require visible AI role and human-owned rationale when AI is used. | AI can become hidden reviewer authority or synthetic consensus. | C7/T8 trace showing source checks and human decision ownership. |
| Public language | Keep claims local and horizon-only until comparative evidence exists. | "Ready", "works", and "generalizes" can outrun evidence. | C8 wording review before any public transfer claim. |

## Minimum Target Evidence Packet

Before any future transfer or pilot discussion, a target-context packet should
answer these questions:

1. What contribution unit would be reviewed?
2. Which evidence can be public, and which evidence cannot safely be exposed?
3. What substitute evidence would preserve auditability if source material is
   private or sensitive?
4. Which record surface would preserve state, rationale, loss notes, and
   contestability without becoming a live policy commitment?
5. Which parts of the source workflow are portable, adaptable, or local-only?
6. Which second-ring dependency is triggered first: governance, rights,
   capture, AI, reward, or transfer language?
7. What would make this target fail before launch?
8. Which public words remain forbidden because C8 is still horizon-only?

The packet would prepare review only. It would not approve a pilot, choose a
target, or create external consequences.

## Safe Local Progress

Safe RQ10 progress from here is still preparatory.

Good local next moves include:

- filling one target-context packet with synthetic values only;
- comparing one candidate record surface for a target type without choosing a
  target;
- mapping one target-specific capture pressure without adopting mitigation;
- separating public evidence from private evidence for one excluded context;
- or adding a public-language boundary check for C8.

Unsafe next moves include:

- selecting a second repo or pilot target;
- claiming the pattern works beyond this repo;
- using governance-package portability as proof of generalization;
- promising rewards, rights, appeals, sanctions, disclosures, or governance
  powers in a target context;
- publishing transfer language;
- or taking any non-GitHub external action.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and require Joe/governance review before:

- naming or committing to a pilot target;
- changing C8 status, public posture, or transfer language;
- adopting transfer criteria as policy;
- exporting governance-package machinery to another repo;
- creating reward, rights, sanction, disclosure, appeal, governance, or
  participant-entitlement commitments;
- assigning legal, financial, civic, client, employment, ownership, token, or
  governance meaning to contribution records;
- or taking legal, civic, client, publishing, deployment, or other non-GitHub
  external action.
