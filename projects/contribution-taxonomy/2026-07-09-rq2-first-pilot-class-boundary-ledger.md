This ledger extends the RQ2 first-pilot class/evidence screen by naming the
specific class-boundary disagreements a later governed review must keep

## Purpose

The class/evidence screen already says the synthetic first-pilot packet is
plausibly `research` primary with `review` as a secondary effect, but not
review-ready until target and source evidence are filled.

The useful next question is:

```text
Which classification disagreements should stay visible before a later reviewer
turns a synthetic first-pilot packet into a live review object?
```

This file answers only at the review-prep level. It gives later review a small
ledger of disagreement labels so missing evidence, mixed class signals, useful
residue, and hidden policy movement are not flattened into one generic
`needs_revision` result.

## Disagreement Ledger

| label | disagreement under review | safe reading | stop before |
|---|---|---|---|
| `primary_class_ordering` | Is `research` primary with `review` secondary, or is the critique/revision effect stronger than the source-backed comparison? | Record the class order and rationale while the packet stays synthetic. | Adopting taxonomy changes, scoring value, accepting the contribution, or creating a live record. |
| `source_evidence_missing` | Does missing target, source set, source/inference split, or limitation make classification impossible? | Route the synthetic result to revision and preserve the exact missing fields. | Treating missing evidence as acceptance, bad faith, low value, or claim support. |
| `mechanism_axis_unclear` | Which mechanism is compared: validation, allocation, voice, contestability, record continuity, or capture resistance? | Ask for one primary mechanism axis before value, prior-art, or rationale review proceeds. | Letting a broad comparison become policy, public proof, or novelty language. |
| `review_vs_policy_movement` | Does the packet critique a scaffold, or does it propose a new rule, role, right, cadence, class, template, or review lane? | Preserve critique as review value and split any policy movement into a governed question. | Editing contribution standards, review paths, reviewer authority, rights, rewards, governance, eligibility, disclosure, or public posture. |
| `useful_residue_boundary` | What remains useful if the packet is narrowed or not accepted as-is? | Preserve a source, comparison axis, failure mode, or class rationale without creating reward or retained-value meaning. | Converting residue into acceptance, future-claim language, reward language, rights language, or log eligibility. |
| `maintenance_semantic_drift` | Would a cleanup or synthesis edit preserve meaning, or would it change review-state, class, acceptance, or rationale meaning? | Treat meaning-preserving cleanup separately from semantic drift. | Bundling promises, authority, review states, or acceptance language into maintenance. |
| `tool_support_authority` | Would an implementation or AI-assisted summary support review, or would it decide legitimacy, class, score, novelty, or evidence sufficiency? | Keep tools and AI as aids with human-owned rationale and visible source trail. | Treating tool output or AI fluency as final authority, evidence, score, or legitimacy. |
| `public_overclaim_risk` | Does the packet use useful evidence to imply that the project has proven contribution legitimacy, fairness, decentralization, transferability, or reward readiness? | Preserve the evidence and remove or quarantine the stronger public-language claim. | Public-posture movement, claim-status movement, test pass/fail movement, transfer claims, or proof language. |

## Minimal Reviewer Record Shape

A later governed review can use this shape without turning the ledger into
policy:

```yaml
packet_id: SAMPLE-RQ5-PACKET-001
classification_disagreement:
  label: <one ledger label>
  primary_class: <candidate class>
  secondary_effect: <candidate secondary effect or none>
  rationale: <public reason>
  missing_evidence: <field list or none>
  useful_residue: <preserved value or none>
  protected_boundary: >
    This classification record is review-prep only. It does not accept a
    contribution, update taxonomy, adopt review paths, assign reviewer
    authority, change standards, create rights or rewards, move claims or
    tests, change public posture, or create a live contribution record.
```

If the reviewer cannot cite a packet field or source surface for each line, the
safe result remains `needs_revision` and `live_review_ready: false`.
