# RQ6 Phase-Transition Evidence Scaffold

Status: review-prep scaffold, not adopted governance.

Purpose: make the current governance phase model reviewable by naming the evidence a later governance review would need before treating any phase transition as legitimate.

This file does not change founder powers, phase definitions, transition triggers, emergency rules, rollback rules, reviewer authority, contribution policy, reward meaning, claim status, public posture, or real contribution records. It converts existing local surfaces into a checklist for later review.

- `GOVERNANCE.md` defines the current founder-led mode and a five-phase governance model.
- `CLAIMS.md` frames C6 as a working claim: founder-led beginnings can be legitimate if powers, limits, and transition milestones are explicit.
- `LEGITIMACY-SCHEMA.md` names visibility, contestability, voice, exit with retained record, adaptive rule change, bounded authority, and non-capture as legitimacy conditions.
- `PROTOCOL-STACK.md` says governance starts founder-led and asks what milestones justify transferring specific powers.

- `projects/founder-phase-constraint/2026-07-02-t7-founder-phase-constraint-review-prep.md` identifies founder-power decision slots and asks for a phase-transition evidence table.

## Review Question

What would the repo need to show before a later reviewer could say a governance phase transition is evidence-backed rather than symbolic?

For this scaffold, "show" means "name observable evidence and stop boundaries." It does not mean a transition is ready, approved, or adopted.

## Evidence Tiers

| tier | meaning | permitted use in later review | not enough for |
|---|---|---|---|
| `review_ready` | The evidence packet is coherent enough for Joe or governance review. | Ask a focused decision question. | Claiming a phase has been completed. |
| `pilot_ready` | The next phase can be tested in a narrow reversible pilot. | Run a bounded pilot with explicit review notes. | Transferring durable authority. |
| `transfer_ready` | A specific power has enough evidence for possible transfer. | Consider power-specific transfer with capture review. | General decentralization claims. |
| `blocked` | Required evidence is missing or capture risk is unresolved. | Preserve the issue as a research gap. | Any governance move. |

These tiers are candidate review vocabulary, not adopted status labels.

## Minimum Review Packet For A Later RQ6 Decision

A later RQ6 review should be able to inspect:

- the phase transition being considered;
- the specific power, process, or review authority affected;
- the evidence tier claimed and why;
- links to contribution decisions, synthetic traces, or review artifacts;
- which legitimacy conditions are improved or stressed;
- one capture-risk note and one overclaim-risk note;
- what remains founder-led after the move;
- whether the move is reversible;
- how contested decisions would be recorded;
- and what would count as failure.

## Early Red Flags

Treat a transition as blocked, not merely incomplete, if:

- it relies on private founder memory to explain why decisions were legitimate;
- it changes who has authority without naming the affected power;
- it uses "community" or "decentralization" language before power actually moves;
- it creates reward or retained-value implications before RQ9 readiness review;
- it creates participant-rights implications before RQ7 review;
- it increases attack profitability without a mitigation or detection path;
- it lacks an adverse-decision or contested-decision trace;
- or it cannot explain how a contributor exits without erasure of accepted work.

## Later Review Prompts

1. Which transition is under review, and which power would actually move?
2. Is the evidence only `review_ready`, or is there a reversible pilot available?
3. Which contribution records or synthetic traces show the current phase working?
4. What adverse or contested decision proves the process can preserve legitimacy under pressure?
5. Which legitimacy condition improves, and which one is most at risk?
6. What new attack becomes profitable if the transition happens?
7. What remains founder-led after the move, and is that visible?
8. What would make the project roll back, narrow, or abandon the move?

## Stop Conditions

Stop and require Joe/governance review before:

- adopting any phase transition, transition trigger, founder limit, founder power, reviewer authority, emergency rule, rollback rule, participant right, or appeal path;
- editing `GOVERNANCE.md`, `GUARDRAILS.md`, `CLAIMS.md`, `CONTRIBUTION-STANDARDS.md`, `CONTRIBUTING.md`, or public posture to reflect this scaffold as policy;
- marking C6, T7, or any governance-transition check passed, failed, or partially passed;
- creating a real governance pilot, reviewer role, transferred power, or public announcement;
- changing reward, retained-value, rights, or real contribution-record meaning;
- or importing governance machinery from another repository as an adopted local rule.
