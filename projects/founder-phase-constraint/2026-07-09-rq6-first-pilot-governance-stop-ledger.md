## Safe Governance Labels

These labels may be used in later review-prep handoffs without adopting policy:

| label | meaning | safe consequence |
|---|---|---|
| `local_governance_trace_repair` | The issue can be clarified by adding affected-power, rationale, source-trace, boundary, or non-adoption language to a draft artifact. | Edit draft review-prep artifacts only. |
| `source_trace_or_record_missing` | The issue cannot be assessed because target, source, reviewer, live record, adverse trace, or contestability evidence is absent. | Keep the packet in revision-prep or evidence-blocked state. |
| `second_ring_governed_stop` | The issue intersects rights, reward, capture, AI, transfer, or governance movement. | Preserve the stop and route to Joe/governed review before live use. |
| `authority_movement_required` | The next action would assign, transfer, constrain, invoke, or rollback authority. | Stop; do not answer in unattended Progress. |
| `public_or_live_use_blocked` | The next action would create public-facing proof, pilot, invitation, contribution-record, or external-consequence meaning. | Stop; keep the artifact local review prep. |
| `do_not_use_live` | The packet would create institutional meaning before evidence and authority boundaries are visible enough. | Do not launch, invite, review, log, score, sanction, reward, transfer, or publish as process. |

These are review-prep labels only. They are not governance states, contribution
review states, rights markers, reward states, transfer states, sanctions, claim
verdicts, or test results.

## What This Adds To The RQ6 Evidence Packet

The earlier governance-evidence packet established that first-pilot scaffolds
can provide packet-shape evidence but not governance-transition evidence. This
ledger adds a narrower operating layer:

1. It classifies newer first-pilot dependencies as governance stops rather than
   treating them as a general second-ring warning.
2. It separates local trace repair from authority movement.
3. It gives later review a compact state vocabulary that can block live use
   without changing policy.

It does not strengthen C6, mark T7 passed, make Phase 1 closer, or show that
founder-led governance is legitimate under real pressure. It only makes the
remaining governance stop conditions easier to inspect.
