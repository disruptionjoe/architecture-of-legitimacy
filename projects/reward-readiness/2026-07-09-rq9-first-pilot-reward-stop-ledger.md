## Safe Reward Labels

These labels may be used in later review-prep handoffs without adopting reward
policy:

| label | meaning | safe consequence |
|---|---|---|
| `local_reward_trace_repair` | The issue can be clarified by adding no-current-reward, score-boundary, loss-note, attribution, or protected-non-conclusion language to a draft artifact. | Edit draft review-prep artifacts only. |
| `baseline_evidence_missing` | The issue cannot be assessed because no live or sufficient synthetic trace shows whether recognition, rationale, and loss notes preserve trust without reward. | Keep the packet in baseline-inspection state. |
| `score_conversion_stop` | The next step would convert value prompts, scores, or rubric language into allocation meaning. | Stop before balance, share, point, payout, or governance-weight language. |
| `retained_value_rights_stop` | The next step would turn retained record, useful residue, loss notes, or future-review language into a right or entitlement. | Stop before rights, ownership, reward debt, or future eligibility. |
| `reward_governance_stop` | The next step would require authority to adopt, pause, revise, or reverse a reward experiment. | Stop for Joe or governed review. |
| `external_meaning_stop` | The next step could create legal, financial, tax, token, ownership, employment, IP, or securities meaning. | Stop; this repo scaffold cannot create or substitute review for those meanings. |
| `public_or_live_use_blocked` | The next action would create public proof, pilot, invitation, contribution-log, contributor-record, or external-consequence meaning. | Stop; keep the artifact as review prep. |
| `do_not_use_live` | The packet would create reward or reward-adjacent meaning before evidence and authority boundaries are visible enough. | Do not launch, invite, review, log, score, rank, reward, transfer, or publish as process. |

These are review-prep labels only. They are not reward states, contribution
review states, rights states, governance states, legal states, claim verdicts,
or test results.

## What This Adds To The RQ9 No-Reward Screen

The earlier no-reward screen established that the synthetic first-pilot packet
can be inspected without reward adoption. This ledger adds a narrower operating
layer:

1. It classifies reward prompts as stop states rather than treating them as a
   general no-reward warning.
2. It separates local wording repair from score conversion, retained-value,
   reward-test, governance, rights, capture, AI, transfer, and external-meaning
   movement.
3. It gives later review a compact state vocabulary that can block live use
   without changing reward policy.

It does not strengthen C4 or C5, mark T5 or RQ9 passed, create reward
readiness, make Phase 1 closer, or show that recognition-only participation is
legitimate under real contributor pressure. It only makes the remaining reward
stop conditions easier to inspect.
