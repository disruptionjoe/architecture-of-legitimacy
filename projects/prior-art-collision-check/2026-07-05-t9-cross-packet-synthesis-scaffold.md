## Shared Pattern Notes

These notes are reviewer synthesis from the packet contents, not source-backed
claims beyond the individual packet evidence.

1. The prepared packets split into two useful review lanes:
   - Funding-allocation lane: Optimism and Gitcoin.
   - Knowledge-validation lane: Wikipedia and academic peer review.
2. All four packets touch modules in this repo's coupled stack, but the
   pressured module differs by lane.
3. Funding-allocation systems are strongest around value allocation, reward
   timing, identity or sybil defense, and allocation-governance mechanics.
4. Knowledge-validation systems are strongest around validation path, record
   trace, contestability, role authority, and public or semi-public rationale.
5. None of the prepared packets yet supplies enough evidence to test the full
   local object as one unit: contribution-level public record, loss-note or
   adverse-decision trace, retained-value semantics, governance transition,
   reward readiness, and capture monitoring bound into one small public
   contribution-allocation protocol.
6. The strongest current narrowing pressure is not "someone already rewards
   public goods" or "someone already validates contributions." The pressure is:
   which parts of the local coupled object are already solved better elsewhere,
   and which parts remain locally under-evidenced?
