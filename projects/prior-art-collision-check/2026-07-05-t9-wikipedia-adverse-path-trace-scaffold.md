## Why This Path

The Wikipedia source packet is strong on public record trace and contestability
vocabulary, but it remains weak on concrete adverse paths. The cross-packet
synthesis names rejected, reverted, disputed, filtered, low-funded, and
under-valued paths as the missing comparison surface. The retained-record
clarification narrows the next question:

```text
Compare how neighboring systems preserve accepted and adverse contribution
paths, and whether the preserved record later does any work beyond recognition.
```

For Wikipedia, the smallest useful next path is:

```text
A contribution is made, reverted or disputed, discussed through visible process,
and ends in a final or current article state that does not simply accept the
original contribution as submitted.
```

## Minimum Evidence Shape

A later adverse-path trace is not ready for comparison unless it has all of:

1. A stable contribution unit, such as a page revision, diff, talk proposal, or
   dispute comment.
2. A visible adverse transition, such as a revert, partial rollback, closure,
   rejection, redirect, deletion, merge, or unresolved dispute.
3. A visible rationale trail connecting the transition to policy, consensus,
   source quality, conduct, neutrality, verifiability, or another named reason.
4. A visible contest surface, or a reason no contest surface is visible.
5. A retained-record statement naming exactly what survives for future review.
6. A retained-value statement separating record, recognition, reward, and
   future eligibility.
7. A source-vs-inference section that does not convert public history into a
   collision verdict by implication.

Label-level references to "Wikipedia has page history" or "Wikipedia has
dispute resolution" are not enough.

## Local Comparison Pressure

If the later trace shows strong public history but weak rationale, the local
burden is not merely "keep records"; it is to preserve why value was lost,
narrowed, or rejected.

If the later trace shows strong discussion rationale but weak future use, the
local burden is to decide whether adverse decisions need loss notes,
retained-value markers, appeal hooks, or only ordinary attribution.

If the later trace shows strong contestability but high policy-fluency costs,
the local burden is to keep a small-repo contribution process reconstructable
without requiring private founder context or expert process navigation.

If the later trace shows recognition without reward, it should pressure this
repo's reward-readiness and retained-value questions without pretending that
Wikipedia already solves reward allocation.
