## Directly Supported Versus Inferred

Directly supported by local packet contents:

- Python uses public pull requests, tests, review comments, and updates as part
  of contribution workflow.
- Core-team members hold merge authority and assess readiness, compatibility,
  documentation, CLA status, tests, labels, branch target, and related
  discussions.
- The triage team can classify issues and PRs, review PRs, assist contributors,
  and has bounded closure authority.
- Core-team membership is based on sustained quality contribution and includes
  a vote plus steering-council non-veto.
- Python's current governance model is a steering-council model selected after
  the BDFL period.
- PEP 13 names conflict-of-interest limits, no-confidence paths, core-team
  voting, council constraints, and exceptional ejection mechanisms.

Reviewer inferences preserved for later review:

- Python may already solve parts of contribution trace, reviewer authority, role
  transition, and founder-transition visibility better than a small public
  research repo.
- Python may still be weak for local purposes where adverse contribution paths,
  loss notes, retained-value ambiguity, reward-readiness boundaries, and
  non-code contribution recognition need visible source traces.
- A later T9 verdict may need to compare Python as maintainer-governance overlap
  rather than as reward-allocation overlap.
