## Second-Wave Pattern Notes

These notes synthesize existing packets. They are not source claims beyond the
underlying packets.

1. The first four packets split into funding-allocation and knowledge-validation
   lanes. The Python packet adds a maintainer-governance lane, while the commons
   packet adds an institutional-design lane.
2. The strongest current pressure on local novelty is no longer only public
   goods funding or knowledge validation. It now includes mature examples of
   bounded project authority, contributor role transition, and participant-made
   rule systems.
3. The local differentiator, if it survives later review, is likely narrower
   than "legitimacy" or "governance." It may be the attempt to bind visible
   contribution evidence, adverse-path loss notes, retained-record semantics,
   reward-readiness boundaries, founder transition, and capture checks into a
   small public contribution-allocation protocol.
4. The Python and commons packets make C6 governance transition and bounded
   authority more exposed than the earlier four-packet synthesis showed.
5. The funding packets still create the strongest reward-timing pressure. The
   knowledge-validation and maintainer-governance packets create stronger
   record-trace and review-authority pressure. The commons packet creates the
   strongest formal legitimacy-conditions pressure.
6. All six packets remain incomplete for verdict purposes because adverse paths,
   rejected or under-valued work, and retained-value semantics remain unevenly
   evidenced.

## Candidate Next Review Shape

The lowest-risk verdict-preparation step is not a full T9 collision judgment. It
is a path-specific adverse-trace comparison.

Useful candidate shapes:

1. Python: one closed, delayed, or heavily revised pull request or issue,
   including visible rationale and any retained contribution record.
2. Commons governance: one concrete commons case or IAD-coded example, including
   how participants, rules, monitoring, conflict resolution, and outcomes are
   recorded.
3. Gitcoin or Optimism: one rejected, low-funded, sybil-flagged, or
   under-rewarded application path, including what record and future value
   survive.
4. Wikipedia or peer review: one reverted edit, disputed contribution,
   desk-rejected manuscript, or rejected-after-review path, including visible
   rationale and later record effects.

Any one of those would make the next T9 packet sharper without crossing into a
claim-status or public-posture decision.
