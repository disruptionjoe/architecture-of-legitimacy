## Boundary Under Review

The synthetic first-pilot packet is intentionally low-governance: no real
contributor, no live source set, no live record surface, no review owner, and
no contribution-log entry. That makes it safe for local review-prep, but it can
hide a capture problem:

```text
a later live first-pilot packet may need capture observability before the repo
has adopted capture-response policy
```

The safe current move is to name what a first-pilot packet should make visible
and where the review must stop. It is not to decide how the repo will respond
to capture in live cases.

## Minimum Capture Fields For A Later Packet

These are review prompts, not active template requirements:

| field | question it preserves | boundary it must state |
|---|---|---|
| `target_selection_rationale` | Why is this target an appropriate first-pilot object? | Target choice is not proof, transfer, or public-posture movement. |
| `capture_observation` | What capture, conflict, volume, AI, reward, rights, or governance pressure is visible from the record? | Observation is not accusation, sanction, or policy. |
| `source_trace` | Which claims are source-backed, and which remain inference? | Missing source evidence does not become a novelty or collision verdict. |
| `ai_role_note` | Was AI used for orientation, drafting, checklist comparison, or red-team prompts? | AI is not final authority, hidden evidence, score, or reviewer. |
| `beneficiary_loss_note` | Who would benefit or lose if this packet were accepted, narrowed, or rejected? | The note does not decide conflicts, eligibility, rewards, or rights. |
| `local_repair_candidate` | What visibility or rationale improvement could reduce `P_a` or increase `D_a` without policy adoption? | Repair is not monitoring, disclosure, sanction, or reviewer-authority policy. |
| `escalation_boundary` | Which next action requires Joe or governed review? | The packet must stop before institutional meaning is created. |

A later governed template change may accept, reject, rename, or replace these
fields. Until then, they belong in research scaffolds and synthetic review only.

## Local Repair Versus Escalation

Safe local research work can:

- add visibility to synthetic packet fields;
- name which RQ8 variable a packet pressure affects;
- separate observed record from inference and benign explanation;
- preserve source trails, uncertainty markers, and non-adoption language;
- compare local repair candidates without adopting them;
- or identify the exact Joe/governed review point before live use.

Stop for Joe or governed review before:

- requiring disclosures, identity linkage, AI-use declarations, conflict forms,
  monitoring, rate limits, eligibility limits, rejection rules, sanctions, or
  public labels;
- assigning reviewer authority, founder powers, emergency powers, rollback
  rights, appeal paths, dispute process, participant rights, retained-value
  rights, reward logic, score conversion, governance weight, or transfer
  meaning;
- editing `THREAT-MODEL.md`, `CLAIMS.md`, `GOVERNANCE.md`, `GUARDRAILS.md`,
  contribution standards, issue templates, live review policy, contribution-log
  schema, public posture, or real contribution records;
- marking C4, T6, RQ8, capture resistance, or any test as passed, failed, or
  stronger;
- using synthetic attack scenarios as evidence about any real contributor;
- or taking any non-GitHub external action.
