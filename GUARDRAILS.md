# Guardrails

This project is intentionally ambitious. Guardrails keep ambition from becoming overclaim.

## Not Claimed

This project does not currently claim:

- to solve governance,
- to prove a post-scarcity political economy,
- to make AI judgment neutral,
- to make open participation automatically fair,
- to make capture impossible,
- to demonstrate that collaboration already dominates defection,
- to have a mature reward system,
- to be decentralized today,
- or to have proven that one repo can generalize to broader institutions.

## Current Claim Boundary

The present-tense claim is:

> A public repo can serve as a bounded proving ground for studying whether legitimacy, contribution accounting, reward rights, and governance transitions can be designed as one coupled architecture.

## Failure Honesty

The project should be willing to publish negative results.

Examples:

- the contribution workflow is too burdensome,
- value rubrics are too subjective to remain legitimate,
- open attemptability creates too much noise,
- founder-led governance cannot maintain trust,
- rewards distort contribution quality,
- attack modeling shows capture becomes easier with growth,
- or prior art already solves the problem better.

## AI Guardrails

AI may be used for:

- critique,
- synthesis,
- rubric testing,
- prior-art exploration,
- attack-surface ideation,
- and drafting proposals.

AI should not be treated as:

- neutral authority,
- final judge,
- source of legitimacy,
- replacement for participant voice,
- or a way to hide founder preference behind technical language.

## Language Guardrails

Use "hypothesis," "research program," "candidate schema," and "proving ground" when claims are not yet established.

Avoid:

- "solves governance,"
- "proof of fairness,"
- "decentralized" before power has actually moved,
- "neutral AI,"
- and "post-scarcity institution" as if the project has already built one.
