# Architecture Of Legitimacy Discovery Run

Status: completed.

Run family: Repo Discovery Run.

Target repo: Architecture Of Legitimacy.

## Objective

Identify what the contribution-allocation research repo may be missing, underweighting, or overcommitting to.

## Required Reads Completed

- `AGENTS.md`
- `steward/README.md`
- `RESEARCH-AGENDA.md`
- `ROADMAP.md`
- `CLAIMS.md`
- repo top-level structure and git status

## Findings

1. The repo has a clear research agenda and claim ledger. Its strongest novelty candidate is not any single mechanism, but the coupled protocol stack around contribution, legitimacy, reward rights, and governance transition.
2. The central test burden is Phase 1: make one valid external contribution possible without private context.
3. There is a risk of second-ring topics such as reward logic, governance transition, and generalization pulling attention before the first contribution workflow is tested.
4. AI-use and legitimacy remain carefully bounded: AI may support judgment, critique, synthesis, and review, but cannot supply legitimacy.

## Non-Binding Recommendations

- Future Progress should prioritize the smallest end-to-end contribution workflow dry run.
- Future Discovery should adversarially test whether the coupled-stack novelty survives prior art in open source, grants, peer review, DAOs, and commons governance.
- Keep reward and transition language horizon-level until the workflow produces evidence.

## Stop Conditions

- No claim statuses or governance documents changed.
- No public-consequence work performed.

## Receipt

Discovery clarified that the repo's highest leverage is proving the first contribution workflow, not expanding the governance/reward theory ahead of evidence.
