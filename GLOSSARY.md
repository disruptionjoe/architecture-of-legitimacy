# Glossary

## Architecture Of Legitimacy

The proposed coupled design object: rules, rights, review, reward, and governance structures that let contribution allocation remain opt-in, contestable, adaptive, and capture-resistant.

## Capture Resistance

The property of making economic, epistemic, or governance capture less profitable or less durable as the system grows.

## Contribution Capital

A provisional term for durable value created through contribution to a shared project. This may include recognition, rights, future claim, governance relevance, or other forms not yet formalized.

## Contribution Ledger

A record of contributions, validation status, value notes, and possible retained claims. In this project, the ledger begins as a contribution log, not a financial instrument.

## Defection

Behavior that extracts value from the system while weakening truth-seeking, legitimacy, contribution quality, or governance integrity.

## Founder-Led Phase

The initial governance phase in which the founder retains final authority while making decisions visible, contestable, and increasingly constrained.

## Legitimate Contribution Allocation

The process of recognizing, evaluating, and rewarding contributions in a way participants experience as acceptably fair, visible, contestable, and non-coercive.

## Open Attemptability

The principle that anyone may attempt to contribute if they follow the rules, without assuming every attempt is valid, accepted, rewarded, or governance-bearing.

## Post-Scarcity

A condition or direction in which some forms of production become less constrained by scarcity, shifting more importance onto attention, trust, judgment, legitimacy, and value allocation.

## Progressive Decentralization

A staged transfer of specific powers away from founder or central control. In this project, it is legitimate only if milestones, powers, and constraints are explicit.

## Protocol Stack

The coupled set of contribution, validation, reward, rights, review, governance, and capture-monitoring rules being studied.

## Retained Value

The idea that contributors should not lose the record or future relevance of accepted work merely because they exit the project.
