# Standing-first intervention boundary observation

## Source

CAI Systemic Failure commit `2b53419` compares intervention and correction
classes across five admitted records while preserving each domain's control
over remedy meaning and evaluation.

## Bounded observation

The comparison does not support one transferable intervention. It does expose
a narrower recurring legitimacy shape:

1. changed or contradictory evidence must become visible;
2. an accountable owner must be answerable for a response;
3. affected people or systems retain standing; and
4. a correction, escalation, retest, or stop route remains available.

The domain boundary is load-bearing. Aviation, spaceflight, drinking water,
offshore engineering, and building safety do not receive interchangeable
remedies merely because their review chains share this shape.

## Distinction from the revision-pin case

The cross-owner revision-pin observation concerns how two repositories preserve
source-correction authority, receiver disposition authority, and historical
basis. This case concerns who retains standing and why a generic process shape
cannot authorize a domain remedy. The mechanisms complement rather than repeat
one another.

## Limits and recheck

This is one repository's bounded synthesis over retrospective public records.
It does not establish remedy effectiveness, participant rights, a general
standing theorem, governance policy, K1 movement, or a public-posture change.

Recheck if a source-record correction changes the common residue, or if an
independent domain supplies a contrary case in which legitimate correction
does not require one of the four elements above.
